# PSS Orange Flagship Projects — Case Study Descriptions

## 1. Vardhaman Steel (55 MW, Punjab, India)
**Short Description (for modal):**
India's first large captive solar power plant for a green steel manufacturer. Vardhaman Steel is expanding production while reducing energy costs by 30% and carbon footprint by 50,000 tonnes annually. The project demonstrates our ability to finance and build utility-scale projects for industrial decarbonization.

**Long Description (for detail page):**
Vardhaman Steel sought to decarbonize its steel production while reducing operational energy costs. PSS Orange developed, financed, and built a 55 MW ground-mounted solar facility to supply power directly to the steel plant as a captive facility. The project reduces grid dependency and locks in predictable energy costs for the next 25 years. Annual generation: ~90 GWh. CO2 avoided: 50,000 tonnes/year. Off-taker: Captive (Vardhaman Steel). Status: In development / Completion Q2 2025.

## 4. Seacon PLC - Srinakharin (6 MW, Bangkok, rooftop)
**Short Description (for modal):**
Bangkok's largest commercial rooftop solar installation. Seacon demonstrates our ability to design and execute complex rooftop projects on premium retail real estate while delivering cost savings and sustainability goals for enterprise clients.

**Long Description (for detail page):**
Seacon PLC operates one of Southeast Asia's largest shopping mall chains. Their Srinakharin branch needed to reduce energy costs and carbon footprint while maintaining premium customer experience. PSS Orange designed and installed a 6 MW rooftop solar system using bifacial panels and advanced monitoring to optimize performance in Bangkok's tropical climate. The installation reduces Seacon's grid draw by 35% and energy costs by 40 million THB annually. The project showcases our expertise in complex architectural integration, high-reliability O&M in urban environments, and delivering measurable ROI for C&I clients. Annual generation: ~9 GWh.

## 5. Maxion Wheels (3 MW, Saraburi, Thailand)
**Short Description (for modal):**
First dual-source energy installation combining grid, solar, and battery storage. Maxion Wheels reduced energy costs by 40% while securing energy independence for a global automotive components manufacturer.

**Long Description (for detail page):**
Maxion Wheels, a global automotive components supplier, needed predictable energy costs and supply security. PSS Orange designed a hybrid system: 3 MW ground-mounted solar + battery storage + grid connection, allowing Maxion to optimize between self-generation, storage, and grid purchasing throughout the day. The system reduces operational energy costs by 40%, improves power quality, and provides resilience during grid disturbances. This was PSS Orange's first demonstration of integrated solar-storage systems in Thailand, a model now deployed across our portfolio. Annual generation: ~4.5 GWh.

## 6. IRPC (12 MW, Rayong, floating solar)
**Short Description (for modal):**
Thailand's first large-scale floating solar project. IRPC demonstrates our innovation in water-based renewable energy and our ability to execute complex engineering projects at utility scale.

## 7. Prime Road (18 MW, Various Locations, Thailand)
**Short Description (for modal):**
Thailand's first major portfolio repowering project. Prime Road demonstrates our ability to extend the economic life of aging solar assets and unlock value from legacy installations.

**Long Description (for detail page):**
Prime Road is a portfolio of older solar installations across Thailand that had degraded panel performance and aging equipment. Rather than decommissioning, PSS Orange undertook a comprehensive repowering: replacing aged panels with high-efficiency models, upgrading inverters and control systems, and implementing advanced predictive O&M. The repowered portfolio restored capacity from 70% to 95% nameplate rating, extending economic life by 10+ years. This project pioneered a new service line for PSS Orange: helping aging asset owners unlock second-generation returns without major capex. Total capacity after repowering: 18 MW (across multiple sites). Aggregate CO2 avoided over extended life: 150,000+ tonnes.

## 8. Khon Kaen University (30 MW, Khon Kaen, Thailand)
**Short Description (for modal):**
World's largest solar installation on a university campus. Khon Kaen demonstrates our ability to execute mega-scale distributed renewable energy and serve institutional clients with complex technical and sustainability requirements.

**Long Description (for detail page):**
Khon Kaen University commissioned the world's largest solar installation on a single university campus: 30 MW distributed across 47 buildings and open areas. The system reduces the university's grid dependence by 45%, saves ~100 million THB annually in electricity costs, and positions Khon Kaen as a global leader in campus sustainability. PSS Orange designed a custom distributed architecture to account for multiple roof types, load profiles, and administrative buildings, then executed construction with minimal disruption to academic operations. The project required coordination with 47 separate building integrations, custom microgrid logic, and real-time monitoring of 47 separate generation points. Annual generation: ~45 GWh. Status: Completed 2024.

## 9. Naresuan University (12 MW, Phitsanulok, Thailand)
**Short Description (for modal):**
Distributed solar across 52 buildings on one campus. Naresuan demonstrates our expertise in complex, multi-building renewable energy systems and our commitment to supporting institutional decarbonization.

**Long Description (for detail page):**
Naresuan University sought to decarbonize its campus while managing a tight construction timeline and minimizing disruption to student life. PSS Orange designed a distributed 12 MW solar system spanning 52 separate academic and administrative buildings. Rather than a single large installation, the distributed approach allowed parallel construction, faster delivery (60-day timeline), and customized design for each building's structural and electrical profile. The system will generate ~20 million kWh annually, save 395 million THB over 12 years, and reduce campus carbon footprint by 12,000 tonnes/year. The project showcases PSS Orange's ability to manage complexity at scale and deliver on aggressive timelines. Annual generation: ~20 GWh. Status: Completed 2024.
